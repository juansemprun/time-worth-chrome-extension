Use this copy as a ready-to-paste starting point for the Chrome Web Store listing.

## Extension name

TimeWorth

## Short description options

Each option is under the Chrome Web Store short-description limit.

1. See Amazon prices as equivalent working hours based on your salary.
2. Turn Amazon prices into working-time estimates while you shop.
3. Understand what Amazon purchases cost in hours of your work.
4. Compare Amazon prices to your real working time, not just money.

## Long description

TimeWorth helps you understand Amazon prices in a more personal way: your time.

Instead of seeing only the product price, TimeWorth shows an estimated working-time equivalent based on your salary, salary period, currency, and weekly work hours. When you browse supported Amazon sites, the extension adds a small badge near product prices so you can quickly see about how many hours of work an item represents.

Use TimeWorth to shop more intentionally, compare purchases against your real earning time, and make spending decisions with clearer context.

### How it works

1. Open the TimeWorth popup.
2. Choose your currency.
3. Enter your salary type, salary amount, and weekly work hours.
4. Browse Amazon and see price badges converted into estimated work hours.

TimeWorth supports English and Spanish automatically based on your browser language.

## Feature bullets

- Converts Amazon product prices into estimated working hours.
- Uses your salary amount, salary period, weekly hours, and currency.
- Adds a lightweight badge next to supported Amazon price displays.
- Updates calculations when your settings change.
- Supports English and Spanish UI automatically.
- Stores settings with Chrome sync so they can follow your browser profile.
- No account, backend, analytics, or tracking required.

## Privacy and data-use wording

TimeWorth stores only the settings required for its calculations: currency, salary type, salary amount, and weekly work hours. These settings are saved using Chrome's `storage.sync` feature.

The extension reads visible Amazon price text locally in your browser to calculate working-time equivalents. TimeWorth does not sell data, does not share data with external servers, does not include analytics, and does not track browsing behavior.
